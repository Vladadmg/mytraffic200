MyTraffic 2004 FAQ

How is MyTraffic 2004 different from MyTraffic for FS2002?

Firstly, MyTraffic 2004 is FS2004 compatible. It has several thousand more flight plans than earlier versions of MyTraffic and includes several new models of aircraft and liveries. In the current version there are:

  • 500000+ flight plans

  • 5500 + airports

  • 62 different aircraft models

  • 1080+ paints

Furthermore, the number of airports which MyTraffic co-ordinates traffic between has been increased.

Is the mechanism of traffic in FS2004 the same as FS2002? How have these changes affected MyTraffic?

FS2004 handles AI traffic differently to FS2002. In addition to improvements in traffic movements in the air and on the ground, FS2004 has a new location where traffic data is stored: [FS2004] \Scenery\World\scenery

FS can now handle multiple bgl files containing AI traffic data. Hence it is possible to use almost any AI add-on along with MyTraffic.

This means that in order to modify ground behavior like adding gates, at least parts of the airports have to be completely changed. Once an SDK reveals details we expect add-on airports to appear featuring improved traffic behavior as opposed to the default airports, preventing cargo and military aircraft from parking at passenger terminals. The MyTraffic team will participate in this process and provide add-on airports optimized for traffic. We will help other add-on developers to get their airports compatible with FS2004 taxiway networks
