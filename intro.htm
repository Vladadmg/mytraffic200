The Concept
AI Traffic is a feature which has become an integral part of simulated flying. You are no longer alone, aircraft taxi on the ground and fly between the airports. The default AI Traffic, however, is severely lacking in realism. Limited to a handful of fictional airlines, by default, most international airports are deserted or show traffic that may have been realistic 40 years ago.

MyTraffic, a self-contained package populating the FS2004 world with realistic traffic, real airlines, more than 60 aircraft types.. By associating airlines with particular regions, hubs and countries, we have made it possible to view the right airlines in the right regions, without making them boring to see one airline only all day.

FS2004, as it is today, allows AI traffic into 5500 airports. Only about 1000 of these ever are visited by airliners like the 737, another thousand see regular commuters, but most of them are General Aviation air fields. Airliners only are a small part of any realistic traffic product for FS2004. MyTraffic 2004 takes this into account by adding many new General Aviation aircraft models, you happen to see three of them here at S62.

Containing many aircraft in about thousand of liveries, MyTraffic contains airlines and aircrafts from all regions across the globe. The textures have been created to minimize the impact on frame rates, finding a balance between realism and performance. More than that, MyTraffic also contains military aircraft and General aviation aircraft - in real world traffic airliners and commuters count for less than 20% of all air traffic.

MyTraffic comes to you as a complete software family. The base edition will bring real world life to the airports. An additional design tool will allow modification of the traffic pattern, addition of new planes and individual schedules. Smaller add-on modules will feature new aircraft, repaints, new airlines and, once it can be done, extended airports to support more traffic, air modified airports to show traffic.

MyTraffic 2004 base edition consists of three main parts:

The Traffic BGL File: This file contains all the data (flight plans) for the AI Traffic. With MyTraffic installed, FS2004 simulates 465000 flights per day.

The Aircraft and Aircraft Repaints: This package includes repaints of the default FS2004 planes and 41 additional aircraft, each painted in several airline liveries. MyTraffic 2004 does more than 300 real airlines, a complete list can be found at the bottom of this text. The following table shows the aircrafts included in the MyTraffic 2004. With all the variations, including the unique registration number for each aircraft, there are approximately 17300 simulated AI aircraft in MyTraffic. In other words: one half of the worlds jetliner fleet, and nice fraction of the military and general aviation fleets.

General Remarks about Traffic in FS2004: Contrary to FS2002 where the behaviour of aircraft on ground could be manipulated by AFD layout files, the behaviour in FS2004 is integral part of the airports. This is very good news in most cases, since most of the 5500+ airports in MyTraffic are well equipped by Microsoft to sustain decent traffic. While many airports will see more traffic than in MyTRaffic 1.3 for FS2002, some of the bigger airports however are underequipped and might see little less traffic. Since MyTraffic adjusts the schedules to the capacities of the airports, this should not trouble you unless you try to force them beyond their capacities. The bad news in this is that a part of the militray airports that had traffic in MyTRaffic 1.3 are abandonned in the moment.

Since any Third Party Airport compatible to FS2004 must contain these active parts, any layout incompatibilities are excluded. Work on active airports will start once the Scenery SDK gets published by Microsoft
